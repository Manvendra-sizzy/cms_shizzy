9.  POSH – Prevention of Sexual Harassment & Workplace Safety

The Company has zero tolerance for any form of sexual harassment or gender-based misconduct. It strictly adheres to the provisions of the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (“POSH Act”) and the associated Rules, 2013.

2.Definition of Sexual Harassment
For the purpose of this Agreement, “sexual harassment” includes any unwelcome physical, verbal, non-verbal, or digital conduct of a sexual nature that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment, including but not limited to:
○ Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favours,

○ Sexually coloured remarks or jokes,

○ Display or transmission of sexually explicit material,

○ Any physical conduct or gesture of a sexual nature,

○ Inappropriate messages, emails, images, or social-media communication.

3.Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
The Company has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) as

required under the POSH Act.

○ The ICC comprises a Presiding Officer, an external legal/NGO member, and two employee members.

○ Employees can file complaints confidentially via the official POSH email
/ portal / HR in-person.

○ The ICC will investigate and recommend appropriate action within statutory timelines.

4.Duties of the Employee

○ Maintain dignity, respect, and professionalism toward all colleagues, clients, and vendors.

○ Cooperate fully in any inquiry conducted by the ICC.

○ Refrain from behaviour or comments that could constitute harassment, bullying, or retaliation.

5.Duties of the Company

○ Provide a safe and secure workplace, including safe commuting arrangements when required.

○ Conduct periodic POSH awareness and training sessions.

○ Ensure every complaint is handled confidentially, impartially, and
without retaliation.

6.Consequences of Violation
Any employee found guilty of sexual harassment, whether through the ICC process or internal disciplinary review, shall face strict disciplinary action,

which may include termination of employment, along with reporting to competent legal authorities under Section 509 of the Indian Penal Code.

7.Protection from Retaliation
No employee shall be victimized or discriminated against for filing or supporting a POSH complaint in good faith.

8.Awareness & Acknowledgment
By signing this Agreement, the Employee confirms having read and understood the Company’s POSH Policy, and agrees to abide by its provisions at all times.

○ The Employee shall not hold any financial interest in a competing business or act as an advisor, director, or consultant to such entities while employed.

3.Limited Post-Employment Restriction (6 Months)

○ Accept employment with, consult for, or provide services to any direct competitor of the Company in India, where such role would involve use or disclosure of the Company’s confidential information or trade secrets.

○ Establish or assist in establishing a business substantially similar to the Company’s core business operations (including digital marketing, web development, software, and business consulting) where confidential information could reasonably be utilized.

For a period of twelve (12) months following cessation of employment, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

○ Solicit or attempt to solicit business from any client, prospect, or vendor of the Company with whom the Employee had material contact during employment; or

○ Divert or attempt to divert any business opportunity that belongs to or was being pursued by the Company.

6.Non-Solicitation of Employees and Contractors
For a period of twelve (12) months after termination, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

○ Induce, recruit, or attempt to persuade any current employee, consultant, or contractor of the Company to leave their position; or

○ Engage or employ any person who was employed or contracted with the Company within the previous six months.

7.Confidentiality Linkage
The Employee acknowledges that these restrictions are necessary to protect the Company’s confidential information, trade secrets, client goodwill, and workforce stability. Any breach shall be deemed a material violation of the

confidentiality and ethical obligations under this Agreement.

8.Reasonableness and Enforceability
The Employee agrees that these restrictions are reasonable in scope, duration, and geography, intended solely to protect the Company’s legitimate interests and not to prevent gainful employment elsewhere.

The Company shall be entitled to injunctive relief, damages, or both, to prevent or remedy any breach of this clause, in addition to other remedies available under law.

15.  Conflict of Interest
a.Definition and Policy
A conflict of interest arises when an Employee’s personal, financial, or other interests interfere — or appear to interfere — with the Employee’s ability to act in the best interests of the Company.
The Employee is expected to act with honesty, integrity, and complete objectivity in all professional dealings, avoiding any conduct that may compromise or appear to compromise impartial judgment.

2.Prohibited Conflicts
The Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:
